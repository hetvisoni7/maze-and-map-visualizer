How to Use

Blind Search

  • DFS — Explores depth-first using a stack. May not find shortest path.
  • BFS — Explores breadth-first using a queue. Guarantees shortest path.
  • DB-DFS — DFS with a configurable depth limit to avoid infinite loops.

Heuristic Search

  • Hill Climbing — Always moves to the neighbour closest to the goal (Manhattan distance). May get stuck in local optima.
  • Tabu Search — Like hill climbing but keeps a tabu list to escape local optima.
  • Iterated HC — Restarts hill climbing from random positions to escape local optima.
  • Beam Search — Keeps $b$ best candidates at each level to explore multiple paths.

Stochastic Search

  • Random Walk — Moves randomly to explore the maze, keeping track of the best node found.
  • Stochastic HC — Decides moves based on probability and temperature (T).
  • Simulated Annealing — Uses cycles and epochs with a cooling schedule to refine the search.
